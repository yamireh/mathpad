# MathPad — Creative & Advertising Brief

A handover doc for the ad/creative team. Everything needed to plan photos,
videos, screenshots, and messaging.

## 1. One-liner

**MathPad — practice math by hand.** A calm, premium iPad/iPhone app where kids
(ages ~5–12) solve real math problems with their finger, just like on paper —
carrying, borrowing, long division, and showing their working.

## 2. What it is

Most math apps are tap-the-right-answer quiz games. MathPad is the opposite: it
recreates **pencil-and-paper math**. Kids **handwrite each digit**, the app
**recognizes their handwriting**, and they work problems out in a notebook-style
**scratch area** — carrying tens, borrowing, lining up long division. It teaches
the *method*, not just the answer. No ads, no noise, no pressure — a focused,
encouraging place to build real math fluency.

## 3. Who it's for

- **The user:** children roughly **5–12**, early primary through pre-teen.
- **The buyer:** **parents** — specifically the "premium-education" parent who
  already invests in tutoring, Kumon, good schools, enrichment. They compare
  MathPad to "an hour of tutoring," not to a $0.99 game. They value
  **handwriting + showing work**, **privacy**, and a **calm, ad-free** experience.

## 4. Why it's different (the selling points)

- ✍️ **Write by hand, not multiple choice.** Real handwriting recognition; kids
  form the digits themselves.
- 📝 **Show your working.** A built-in notebook "worksheet" scratch area for
  carrying, borrowing, and long-division steps.
- 🧮 **Real paper methods.** Column addition/subtraction with carry/borrow,
  multi-step multiplication, full **long division** with bring-downs.
- 👆 **"Watch how to solve."** An animated hand demonstrates each method
  step-by-step — a friendly teacher inside the app. *(Great hero footage.)*
- 💡 **Gentle hints, not answers.** One tap reveals just the next step.
- 🧘 **Calm by design.** No ads, no flashing rewards, no timers-of-doom, no
  "limited offer" anxiety. Encouraging, not addictive.
- 🔒 **Private & offline.** Works fully offline; collects **no personal data**.
  A genuine parent reassurance and an honest App Store "Data Not Collected"
  badge.

## 5. Key features (for copy points)

- Four operations: **Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication, Division** + a
  **Mix** challenge mode.
- Adjustable difficulty: number of digits, number of questions, optional timer,
  and options like decimals, remainders, and negative answers.
- Handwriting recognition + finger-writing on a notebook-grid surface.
- Step-by-step **How to solve** walkthroughs per operation.
- Progressive **hints**.
- A friendly **results** screen (first-try score, final score, accuracy) with
  encouraging copy, and a **review/fix** flow.
- **History** of past sessions.
- iPhone + iPad. (Android planned.)

## 6. Brand & visual identity

- **Name:** MathPad. **Tagline:** "Practice math by hand."
- **Icon/art:** a friendly 3D **abacus** (colorful beads) — warm, tactile,
  unmistakably "math, by hand."
- **Look:** clean, premium, **calm**. Soft white rounded cards, gentle shadows,
  lots of breathing room, a light background (`#F7F8FA`). Think "nice notebook +
  Apple-clean," not "arcade."
- **Signature texture:** a faint **notebook grid** behind the writing/worksheet
  areas — handwriting in dark ink on grid paper is the iconic visual.
- **Color system — one accent per operation** (use these to color-code creative):
  - Addition — **blue** `#3B82F6`
  - Subtraction — **coral** `#FF6F61`
  - Multiplication — **purple** `#8B5CF6`
  - Division — **teal** `#14B8A6`
  - Mix — **deep navy/indigo** `#312E81`
  - Correct = **green** `#22C55E`; hints/"how to" highlight = **amber** `#F59E0B`.
- **Type:** clean system sans-serif (San Francisco / Roboto), two weights, very
  legible — never childish/comic fonts.

## 7. Tone & personality

Warm, calm, encouraging, premium. "A patient teacher," not a loud game. Speaks to
proud, busy, quality-seeking parents and to curious kids. Confident and simple —
no hype, no gimmicks.

## 8. What to capture (screens & moments)

**Hero moments (best for video):**
1. A child's **finger writing a digit** and it appearing crisply in the box.
2. The **"Watch how to solve" animated hand** writing out a full solution
   (e.g., long division `512 ÷ 4`) — carrying, borrowing, bringing digits down.
3. The **carry/borrow** animation in column addition/subtraction.
4. The **bring-down** sliding into place in long division.
5. The **celebratory results** screen after a session.

**Screens to screenshot (App Store + ads):**
- Home (colorful topic cards + friendly hero/greeting).
- Operations chooser (the five color-coded operation cards).
- A live **practice** screen mid-solve, with handwriting + the worksheet.
- A **long-division** problem in progress (the most impressive method).
- The **How to solve** demo.
- The **results** screen.

**Photography direction (if shooting real kids):**
- A child at a table with an **iPad**, finger on screen, a calm focused
  expression — natural light, warm home/classroom setting. Show the *hand on
  glass writing numbers*. Avoid stocky "thumbs-up" clichés; aim for genuine
  "in the zone" learning.

## 9. Message angles / hook ideas

- "Math apps make kids tap. MathPad makes them **think** — by hand."
- "The pencil-and-paper method, on iPad."
- "They show their working. You see them learn."
- "Carrying, borrowing, long division — done by hand, the right way."
- "No ads. No noise. No data collected. Just math."
- "Watch a friendly hand teach the steps — then they try it themselves."

## 10. The offer (pricing for the campaign)

- **Free to download. Addition is free, forever** — full features, no demo trap.
- **One-time $9.99** unlocks the other operations (Subtraction, Multiplication,
  Division, Mix). **No subscription.**
- Campaign-friendly framing: **"Try free, unlock once."** Lead with the free
  Addition experience; the upgrade is a single honest purchase, not a recurring
  charge. (Avoid "free trial" language — it's a genuine free tier.)

## 11. Platform & availability

- **iOS (iPhone + iPad)** at launch; Android planned later.
- Targeted at the **Kids** audience (5–12); family-friendly, COPPA-clean.
- **Fully offline**, **no accounts**, **no ads**, **no data collection**.

## 12. Brand guardrails (please keep the campaign on-tone)

**Do:** emphasize *learning real skills*, *handwriting & showing work*, *calm &
ad-free*, *privacy*, *parent trust*, *premium quality*.

**Don't:** frame it as an "addictive game," use fake urgency / countdowns,
flashing-reward / loot-box vibes, babyish cartoon styling, or "cheap app" price
framing. Don't imply data tracking or social features — there are none.
