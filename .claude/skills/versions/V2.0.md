## Goal

Ship a complete, polished **Clock (telling the time)** module that reads an
**analog clock** — matching MathPad's DNA (kids *do* it by hand, no multiple
choice, calm + premium) and reusing the existing shells (settings → practice →
results → review, History, and a "How to solve" demo).

## What's IN V2.0

- **Clock module** — the kid always **reads an analog clock face**; answers in
  one of two formats:
  - **Digital** — handwrite the time (`6:30`) in HH:MM boxes (reuses the
    handwriting recognition engine).
  - **Pattern** — drag word tiles to build the spoken time ("half past six").
  - **Mixed** — alternate the two (default).
- **Settings page** (mirrors the operation settings screen):
  - **Number of questions.**
  - **Type:** Digital · Pattern · Mixed.
  - **Complexity** (minute step): **15s** (`:00/:15/:30/:45`) · **5s** · **Minutes**.
- **Analog clock face** (Skia): numbered face + minute ticks, **realistic
  hour-hand position**, and **training wheels** (count-by-5 ring + color-coded
  hands) that fade as the complexity step gets finer.
- **Pattern/words register in English**, structured so other locales can plug in
  later (the phrase grammar is locale-specific).
- **"How to solve" clock demo** bridging analog ↔ digital ↔ words.
- **Pricing:** Clock as its **own module IAP** per the [[pricing]] skill
  (**$4.99**, set 2026-06-08), with a **free slice** (taster complexity) — exact
  free tier TBD.

## What's NOT in V2.0 (deferred)

- **24-hour / AM-PM** times (answers stay in the 1–12 range; morning/evening is a
  later/advanced option).
- **Set-the-hands** mode (drag the hands to match a given time, snap-to-grid) —
  noted as a natural later addition, not a V2.0 primary.
- **Languages beyond English** for the words tiles (structured for it; not built).
- **Shapes / Axis / Patterns / Money** modules — their own later versions.

## Rules for working in V2.0 scope

1. **Reuse, don't fork.** New surface is the clock + its answer input only; the
   settings/practice/results/history/how-to shells are shared with Operations.
2. **Keep the DNA:** read a real analog face, answer by hand (write or drag
   tiles), calm and ad-free — no multiple choice.
3. **English first, locale-ready.** Put every spoken-time phrase behind i18n
   templates so other languages plug in without rework.
